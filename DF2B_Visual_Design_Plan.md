# DF2B Visual Design Plan

## 1. Design Philosophy

### Overall Visual Direction

Serene, contemplative, and deeply human. The visual direction draws inspiration from the quiet minimalism of *Aesop* and the thoughtful editorial design of *Kinfolk* magazine. The core idea is to create a visual sanctuary that mirrors the app's purpose: a safe, digital space for mental respite. The design uses a muted, sophisticated palette and airy layouts to evoke a sense of calm, clarity, and safety, free from the visual noise of the digital world.

### Content Density

Medium density. Each slide focuses on 1-3 key ideas, allowing for ample white space (around 20-25%) to create a calm, focused atmosphere. This approach prioritizes emotional impact and readability over information overload, aligning with the app's core philosophy of mental clarity and peace.

## 2. Color System

**Primary Colors**

- Background color: #1A202C (Deep slate - represents the night sky and deep ocean, creating a calming dark foundation)
- Primary color: #4A5568 (Soft graphite - for main content areas and containers)
- Secondary color: #2D3748 (Charcoal - for secondary elements and subtle differentiation)
- Accent color: #D69E2E (Warm amber - represents candlelight, hope, and warmth in darkness)

**Text Colors**

- Dark text (for light backgrounds): #1A202C
- Light text (for dark backgrounds): #F7FAFC

## 3. Font Design

**Heading Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Oranienbaum (A modern high-contrast serif with elegant lines, conveying a sense of calm authority and sophistication for main titles)
  - Unna (A neoclassical serif with a strong vertical rhythm, adding a touch of literary grace and contemplation for subtitles)

**Body Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento Sans (A classic, warm, and highly readable sans-serif, ensuring clarity and comfort for all body text)
  - Sorts Mill Goudy (A classic serif with a gentle feel, offering excellent readability and a timeless, trustworthy tone for longer passages)

## 4. Image Style

Imagery should be atmospheric and evocative, focusing on abstract textures, soft-focus natural elements, and quiet, contemplative scenes. Think close-ups of gentle water ripples, soft light filtering through fog, the texture of natural stone, or the warm glow of a single candle in a dark room. Avoid literal or clinical stock photos; instead, use images that evoke a feeling of peace, breath, and safety through mood and metaphor.
